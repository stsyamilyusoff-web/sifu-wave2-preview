Wave 2 · Activate — the new-tutor on-ramp
Redesign brief · before → after · prepared for Hafiz · 23 June 2026
Activate is the funnel feeder for everything in Wave 1: a tutor cannot apply to a request until they are verified. The North Star here is verification-completion rate and time-to-first-application. The live flow is 16 screens, and its bottleneck is the verification wizard. (Note: the live capture was blocked by an OTP throttle on the test number, so the "before" is reconstructed faithfully from the app's code; the verification flow is a form wizard, so this is reliable.)
1 · The bottleneck: a hub, not a flow
Today the wizard is a menu. A tutor taps a step, fills it, gets dumped back to the menu, and hunts for the next one. No "step X of N", no auto-advance. This is the single biggest drop-off driver. The fix is a linear, progress-tracked flow.

3 · The worst friction, fixed
IC validationCryptic "age must be 20-50" rejection becomes inline format help, and the age gate is opened up or explained (see the question below).
Document upload4 files with no preview and vague labels become thumbnail previews, plain examples ("a clear headshot"), and an upload progress bar.
Intro video"Speak clearly, introduce yourself" becomes a short on-screen script prompt + an example, so tutors are not guessing.
Success screen"We will reach out" becomes an honest review-time expectation, then routes straight into the first-job nudge above.
